Problem

Motivation to create a product like Remi sprouted from the team's own habit of working for hours without taking any breaks in front of screens. We then went on to figure out if this really is a burning issue?


"Too much time spent in a chair could shorten our lives, even if we exercise, according to a study that uses objective measures to find the links between lengthy sitting time and death among middle-aged and older adults." - The New York Times

Turned out - it is a common problem. Through formative research we narrowed down our problem focus and target users.

Target Audience: University students and young professionals (specifically those who have a lot of heads down work)
Problem Statement: How can we encourage people to take quick physical activity breaks during long hours of work?

Solution

Remi is a smart cushion that monitors your sitting duration and posture during long hours of work. It estimates which parts of your body might need some stretching and provides you instructions through a companion app. It's light and easy to carry around too. Remi hopes to help you stay active and productive at the same time.

From Insights to Features
We extracted three main insights, developed an initial concept, and translated them into features of Remi.

We debated a lot about features like group breaks, an activity wall, and a network of this product in a shared workplace. Though these features seemed very much feasible, our user feedback highlighted the issue of privacy multiple times.

Insight 1:Users WANT to take breaks during long hours of work. They don’t always REMEMBER to do so. Most people need reminders to increase their awareness of taking breaks.

“I was too busy to notice the time.”

Insight 2: Users didn’t want to be disturbed too many times by a system, while working on something important. Break reminders and activities proposed need to be flexible.

“I might want to snooze this reminder for now.”

Insight 3: Social interactions are complex in a shared office space. The designed product should notify users on a personal level without disturbing others.

“I have a privacy concern.”

Prototyping

We prototyped Remi using Arduino, pressure sensors, vibration motors, fabric, and a woodbox hiding the breadboard under the chair. The final prototyped was created in phases with a simple version which we tested with users and iterated on using feedback received from hallway usability tests.
